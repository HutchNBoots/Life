# App name — resolved

**Decision: Life.**

The original brainstorm (below, unchanged for history) landed on **Ledger** as the recommended pick, reasoning that it described what the app *is* rather than performing positivity. That held for the first design pass. Partway through the visual rework — new palette, new photography reference, full UX pass — the name changed to **Life**, landing in the "more literal register" bucket the original brainstorm also considered and flagged as "as requested."

Practical effect: every reference to "Ledger" as the *app name* in older docs is stale. The word "ledger" lowercase, as a design term (the "ledger page" surface concept — paper-colored cards styled like a page in a physical ledger) is unrelated and still accurate — that visual metaphor didn't change, only the product name.

Repo/domain naming: `life-app`, `golife`, or similar, depending on availability — not decided.

## Original brainstorm (unchanged, kept for history)

Grounded in the ledger/tally identity (not generic "positivity" wordplay) where possible, with a few more literal "stay positive" options since that was explicitly requested.

### Tied to the ledger/tally/scorekeeping concept
- **Ledger** — plain, confident, matches the whole visual identity directly *(earlier pick, since superseded)*
- **Five a Day** — literal (5 items logged), quietly funny echo of the fruit-and-veg slogan
- **The Tally** — names the signature visual element directly
- **Good Ledger** — combines the ritual's purpose with the aesthetic
- **Stamped** — evokes the rubber-stamp/spirit-animal badge motif
- **Scorebook** — leans into the cricket-scorer tally origin of the visual language

### More literal "stay positive" register (as requested)
- **Stay Positive** — direct, does what it says
- **Bright Ledger** — splits the difference between literal and thematic
- **Good Days** — simple, warm, easy to say out loud to the pilot group
- **Three Good Things** — literal reference to the Seligman exercise the app is built on
- **Counted Blessings** — nods to Emmons & McCullough's research framing without being twee

### A few playful ones (given the spirit-animal feature)
- **Streak & Sort** — names both mechanics (streaks + the "to sort" slot) in one phrase
- **Menagerie** — leans fully into the spirit-animal collection as the memorable hook
- **Good Beast** — short, odd enough to be memorable, ties to the spirit animals without being cutesy
